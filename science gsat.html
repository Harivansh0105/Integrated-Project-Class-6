GSAT Satellites

Exploring the Wonders of Indian Space Research

What is GSAT?

GSAT (Geosynchronous Satellite) is a series of communication satellites developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

GSAT Satellites

  • GSAT-1

    Launched in 2001, GSAT-1 was India's first experimental satellite for communication and navigation.

  • GSAT-2

    Launched in 2003, GSAT-2 carried a payload for communication, navigation, and meteorology.

  • GSAT-3

    Launched in 2004, GSAT-3 (also known as EDUSAT) was dedicated to educational services.

  • GSAT-4

    Launched in 2010, GSAT-4 carried a payload for communication, navigation, and scientific experiments.

  • GSAT-5

    Launched in 2011, GSAT-5 (also known as INSAT-4B) provided communication services.

  • GSAT-6

    Launched in 2015, GSAT-6 provided communication services, including mobile communication.

  • GSAT-7

    Launched in 2013, GSAT-7 (also known as INSAT-3D) provided communication services for the Indian Navy. Launched in 2017.

  • GSAT-9

    Launched in 2017, GSAT-9 (also known as South Asia Satellite) provided communication services to South Asian countries

  • GSAT-11

    Launched in 2018, GSAT-11 provided communication services, including broadband connectivity.

  • GSAT-12

    Launched in 2011, GSAT-12 provided communication services.

  • GSAT-29

    Launched in 2018, GSAT-29 provided communication services, including broadband connectivity.

Key Features of GSAT Satellites

  • Communication services for television broadcasting, telecommunications, and broadband connectivity
  • Navigation services for aviation, maritime, and land transportation
  • Meteorological services for weather forecasting and monitoring
